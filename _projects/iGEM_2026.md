I serve as scientific advisor to the University of Sheffield's 2025 iGEM team, whose project focuses on engineered exosomes for blood-brain barrier crossing and targeted therapy in Dravet syndrome. My advisory role spans molecular design, experimental strategy, and scientific communication — including input on construct architecture, delivery mechanism rationale, and framing of the project's translational goals. I work closely with the team to ensure their approach is both scientifically rigorous and grounded in current literature, while supporting their development as independent researchers navigating a complex, interdisciplinary problem space.

The team has raised approximately £18,000 in funding and operates across a genuinely collaborative network, working with multiple faculty members and researchers spanning several universities. This breadth of institutional engagement reflects both the ambition of the project and the team's capacity to build and maintain substantive scientific partnerships; qualities I have been glad to support in my advisory capacity.
