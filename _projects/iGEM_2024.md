I served as scientific advisor to the University of Sheffield's 2024 iGEM team, whose project addressed the global phosphate crisis — the accelerating depletion of inorganic phosphate reserves and the environmental consequences of inefficient phosphate use in agriculture. The team developed a synthetic biology-based phosphate recovery strategy centred on PafA, a phosphatase from Flavobacterium, combined with phosphate-binding proteins (PBPs). Their approach integrated AI-assisted protein redesign (ProteinMPNN and AlphaFold3) to improve PafA expression, thermostability, and pH stability, alongside an immobilised column system enabling simultaneous organophosphate hydrolysis and inorganic phosphate capture - a practical step toward scalable, sustainable phosphate reclamation.

In my advisory capacity, I supported the team in navigating the intersection of molecular engineering, protein biochemistry, and experimental design, helping them develop a scientifically rigorous and well-grounded project from concept through to implementation.
